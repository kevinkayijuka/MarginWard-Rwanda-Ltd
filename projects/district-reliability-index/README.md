# District Reliability Index

**Sector:** Agro-processing / Cooperative sourcing

**Business question:** Which Rwandan districts are the most reliable sourcing zones for a scaling agro-business — not just productive in one season, but consistently productive across multiple years?

**Data source:** NISR Seasonal Agricultural Survey 2020–2025 — Maize, Bean, and Soybean production across all 30 Rwandan districts

**Methodology:** Coefficient of Variation (CV%) calculated on annual production totals per district across 6 years. Lower CV% = more consistent = more reliable as a sourcing zone.

**Key finding:** Districts close to Kigali with the strongest reliability scores:

| District | Distance from Kigali | Avg Annual Production | CV% | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Gakenke | 90km | 52,703 MT | 6.3% | Reliable |
| Ngoma | 90km | 78,100 MT | 9.3% | Reliable |
| Bugesera | 55km | 60,547 MT | 11.8% | Reliable |
| Rwamagana | 55km | 48,832 MT | 10.8% | Reliable |
| Ngororero | 95km | 33,061 MT | 26.3% | Volatile |

**Recommendation:** Agro-businesses and cooperatives scaling operations should prioritize Ngoma and Gakenke as primary sourcing zones — high volume, low variance, within 100km of Kigali. Ngororero despite its proximity is the most volatile district within 100km and should not be relied upon as a primary source.
